About the Survey

This is a survey to help label a public speech dataset as deceptive or truthful. The following pages of the survey contain:

  1. 3 Tutorial Pages: It will give you an idea of how to take the survey
  2. 20 Survey Pages: These are the actual survey questions

How to take the survey

Each page of the survey presents you with a conversation. You read the conversation and indicate in the options below it whether you feel the witness is attempting to be deceptive in their answer. You also indicate how confident you feel regarding your assessment of the given conversation.

What is deception?

"Deception occurs when communicators control the information contained in their messages to convey a meaning that departs from the truth as they know it".

As stated above, we can label a communication as deception if:

  1. There is presence of deliberate intent to deceive
  2. Communicator considers the information to be untrue
  3. It has a purpose, like, gain of esteem, respect, trust or material, or in order to evade judgement that is negative and/or harms them or what they represent.
